## **2. The Foundation: Arkansas Industrial Hemp Act (Act 981)**
### **2.1 Legal Definition of Hemp in Arkansas**
- Cannabis sativa L. with ≤0.3% delta-9 THC on dry weight basis
- Alignment with 2018 Farm Bill federal standard
- Distinction from marijuana under Arkansas law

### **2.2 Key Provisions of Act 981**
- Authorization for cultivation, processing, handling
- Licensing framework establishment
- Research program authorization
- Criminal penalties for unlicensed activities

### **2.3 Legislative Evolution**
- HB 1778 (2017) → Act 981
- Act 629 (2023) - Regulatory alignment updates
- HB 533/SB 533 (2025) - THC limits for consumables

## **3. Licensing Requirements: How to Legally Operate Hemp in Arkansas**
### **3.1 License Types**
- **Grower License**: Commercial cultivation authorization
- **Processor/Handler License**: Receipt, storage, processing of hemp materials
- **Research License**: Academic/agricultural research purposes

### **3.2 Application Process**
- Submission to Arkansas Department of Agriculture
- Site verification with GPS coordinates
- Background checks (criminal history review)
- Application fees and timeline (60-90 days typical)

### **3.3 Annual Requirements**
- License expiration: December 31 each year
- Renewal process and deadlines
- Site modification requirements ($100 fee per change)
- Record-keeping mandates (3-year retention)

## **4. Cultivation Regulations for Arkansas Hemp Farmers**
### **4.1 THC Compliance Framework**
- 0.3% total THC limit (federal alignment)
- Pre-harvest testing requirements
- ADA-approved laboratory testing
- Remediation protocols for non-compliant crops

### **4.2 Site Requirements**
- Registered GPS coordinates for each location
- Site approval before cultivation
- Proximity restrictions (distance from schools, residential areas)
- Security requirements (varies by county)

### **4.3 Production Restrictions**
- Seed/transplant sourcing requirements
- Nurseryman license or phytosanitary certificates for transplants
- Planting density and acreage reporting
- Harvest notification requirements

## **5. Processing & Handling Regulations**
### **5.1 Processor/Handler Requirements**
- Separate license from grower license
- Facility inspection requirements
- Chain-of-custody documentation
- Product testing protocols

### **5.2 Transportation & Sales Restrictions**
- **Prohibition**: "It is prohibited to sell, transport, or market raw hemp materials to the general public or unlicensed entities."
- Licensed-to-licensed transfers only
- Bill of lading requirements
- Interstate commerce compliance

### **5.3 Record-Keeping Mandates**
- Detailed inventory tracking
- Sales documentation
- Testing records (COAs)

## **6. Retail & Consumer Product Regulations**
### **6.1 Dual Regulatory Oversight**
- **ADA**: Cultivation and processing
- **ATCB**: Retail consumable products (since 2025 transfer)
- Compliance requirements for both agencies

### **6.2 THC Limits for Consumable Products (HB 533/SB 533)**
- **1 milligram THC per container** regardless of size
- Impact on CBD and other hemp-derived products
- Product reformulation requirements
- Third-party testing verification

### **6.3 Labeling Requirements**
- Cannabinoid content (mg per serving, mg per container)
- Batch/lot numbers
- Manufacturer information
- Required warnings and disclaimers
- QR code linking to COAs

## **8. Enforcement & Penalties**
### **8.1 ADA Enforcement Authority**
- Inspection powers

- Record review access

### **8.2 Violation Categories**
- **Category 1**: Negligent violations (corrective action plans)

- Retail violations: ATCB enforcement actions
