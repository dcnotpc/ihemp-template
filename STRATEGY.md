# iHemp Network Strategy
Version: 1.0
Last Updated: 2026-03-28
Owner: David Alan Crabill, CEO

## Mission
Educate, advocate, and build commerce around hemp — state by state.

## Vision
Become the most trusted hemp education and commerce network in America.

## Core Values
1. Education over hype
2. Accuracy over speed
3. Transparency always
4. Compliance first
5. Community building
6. Sustainability

## What Makes iHemp Different
- State-specific focus: 16 dedicated state hemp sites
- Education-first model: Content builds trust before sales
- Dual commerce: Merch + CBD under one network
- Advocacy backbone: Non-profit arm adds credibility
- Local relevance: Each site covers that state's laws and news

## We Are NOT
- A dispensary
- A get-rich-quick hemp scheme
- A medical advice platform
- A bulk CBD retailer

## Primary Audiences
- Hemp Enthusiasts: Passionate about hemp culture. Buy merch, share content.
- CBD Wellness Consumers: Seeking natural topical relief. Buy CBD salve.
- Hemp Farmers: Active or aspiring growers. Engage with content.
- Eco-Conscious Shoppers: Sustainability-minded. Buy merch.

## Secondary Audiences
- State Legislators: Policy makers. Advocacy credibility.
- Hemp Industry Pros: Processors, retailers. B2B networking.
- Environmental Advocates: Climate activists. Amplify content.
- Small Business Owners: Entrepreneurs entering hemp. Engage with content.

- Never preachy or salesy
- Speak like a knowledgeable friend, not a professor

- Humor is welcome but never at anyone's expense

## Revenue Streams
1. Merch Sales: State-branded hemp apparel and accessories
2. CBD Salve Sales: Topical CBD products
3. Affiliate Income: Hemp-related product recommendations
4. Sponsored Content: Industry partner articles
5. Future: Online courses, membership community

## Platform Structure
- iHempNetwork.com: Hub site, central brand
- 16 State Sites: iHempMichigan.com, iHempOhio.com, etc.
- Shopify Stores: Merch + CBD commerce
- Social Media: Instagram, Facebook, X, TikTok
- Email Lists: State-specific newsletters
- Non-Profit: iHemp Foundation (advocacy arm)

## Content Strategy
- Education-first: Teach before you sell
- State-specific: Localized laws, news, events
- Evergreen + Timely: Mix of permanent guides and current news
- Content Types: Blog posts, social media, email, video
- Posting Cadence: 2-3 blog posts per week across network

## SEO Strategy
- Target: State + hemp keyword combos
- Examples: "Michigan hemp laws", "Ohio CBD regulations"
- Long-tail focus: Less competition, higher intent
- Each state site targets its own geo-specific keywords
- Internal linking between hub and state sites
- Schema markup for articles and products

## Social Media Strategy
- Instagram: Visual hemp education, merch showcase
- Facebook: Community building, state-specific groups
- X/Twitter: News, advocacy, industry commentary
- TikTok: Short-form hemp education, behind the scenes
- Focus on education and engagement, not hard selling
- Repurpose blog content across all platforms

## Email Strategy
- State-specific lists: Subscribers get local hemp news
- Welcome sequence: Intro to iHemp, what to expect
- Weekly digest: Top stories, new products, events
- Segmentation: By state, by interest (grower, consumer, advocate)
- Always provide value before any sales pitch

## Growth Milestones
- Phase 1: Launch hub site + 3 state sites (MI, OH, IN)
- Phase 2: Add 5 more state sites, launch merch store
- Phase 3: Scale to all 16 states, launch CBD salve line
- Phase 4: Build email lists to 1,000+ per state
- Phase 5: Introduce courses, membership, sponsorships
- Long-term: iHemp becomes the go-to hemp resource network in the US
